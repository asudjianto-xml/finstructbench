### 10.3 Key Risk Findings

1. **Worst capital position** occurs in **Q1 2028** under the Severely Adverse scenario,
   where CET1 falls to **4.7697%** (minimum threshold: 4.5000%).

2. In that quarter (Q1 2028), the highest-loss portfolio segment is **Consumer**
   with losses of **$50.0959M**, representing
   33.0400% of total provisions for that quarter.

3. **CRE concentration risk:** Cumulative CRE losses increase from $48.68M (Baseline)
   to $302.96M (Severely Adverse), a **6.2x** multiplier,
   reflecting the portfolio's sensitivity to commercial property devaluations and rising vacancy rates.

4. **NII erosion under rate compression:** Cumulative NII drops from $3,901.81M (Baseline)
   to $3,156.25M (Severely Adverse), a decline of $745.56M
   (19.1080%), driven by the 10-Year Treasury yield
   declining from 4.2500% to a trough of
   0.9000%.

5. **Capital action flexibility:** Under the Severely Adverse scenario, the institution
   suspends buybacks for 6 quarters and reduces dividends from $65M to $45M/quarter
   for 4 quarters, freeing $320M in capital that partially offsets
   elevated provisions.
