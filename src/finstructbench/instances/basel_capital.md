## Appendix A: Regulatory Framework and Methodology Notes

### A.1 — Basis of Preparation

This report has been prepared in accordance with the Basel III framework as
implemented by U.S. federal banking regulators through the following rules:

- **12 CFR Part 3** (OCC): Risk-Based Capital and Leverage
- **Regulation Q (12 CFR Part 217)**: Capital Adequacy of Bank Holding Companies
- **Basel III Endgame (proposed)**: Expanded risk-based capital requirements

Capital ratios are calculated on a fully phased-in basis. AOCI is included in
CET1 capital without opt-out, consistent with Category IV institution requirements
effective January 1, 2020.

### A.2 — Credit Risk Methodology

Credit risk-weighted assets are calculated using the U.S. Standardized Approach.
Key risk weight assignments follow the Basel III standardized framework:

| Exposure Type | Risk Weight | Regulatory Reference |
|:--------------|:-----------:|:---------------------|
| U.S. Government Securities | 0% | 12 CFR 3.32(a)(1) |
| GSE Obligations | 20% | 12 CFR 3.32(c) |
| Public Sector Entities (U.S.) | 20% | 12 CFR 3.32(e) |
| Corporate (Investment Grade) | 65–100% | 12 CFR 3.32(f) |
| Residential Mortgage (Category 1) | 35–50% | 12 CFR 3.33 |
| Commercial Real Estate (HVCRE) | 150% | 12 CFR 3.33(d) |
| Retail / Consumer | 75–100% | 12 CFR 3.34 |
| Equity Exposures | 100–600% | 12 CFR 3.35 |
| Securitization | 20–1,250% | 12 CFR 3.42 |

### A.3 — Market Risk Methodology

Market risk capital is calculated using the Internal Models Approach (IMA) under
OCC approval (effective March 15, 2019). The VaR model employs historical
simulation with a 500-day lookback period. Stressed VaR uses a stress window
calibrated to the 2008–2009 financial crisis period (September 2008 – March 2009).

### A.4 — Operational Risk Methodology

Operational risk capital is calculated under the Standardised Measurement Approach
(SMA) as prescribed by the Basel III Endgame proposal. The Business Indicator (BI)
is computed from audited financial statements for the prior three fiscal years.
The Internal Loss Multiplier (ILM) reflects the institution's 10-year operational
loss history relative to the BI Component.

### A.5 — Accounting Standards

All figures are presented under U.S. Generally Accepted Accounting Principles
(US GAAP). The Current Expected Credit Losses (CECL) methodology is applied for
the allowance for credit losses, with a three-year phase-in adjustment for
regulatory capital purposes (75% phase-in as of the reporting date).
